Where Open Notebook Wins

1. Privacy & Compliance

With Ollama, documents never leave your network. For legal, healthcare, proprietary code, and classified material, this is a hard requirement that NotebookLM can't meet. Even with cloud APIs, only query text goes out — your source documents stay on your server.

2. No Source Caps

NotebookLM's 50-source, 500K-word limit is the #1 reason users search for alternatives. Open Notebook has no arbitrary caps — the limit is your storage and context window. Upload 500 PDFs, 200 URLs, 50 hours of audio. It all indexes.

3. API & Automation

Open Notebook exposes a full REST API. You can programmatically ingest documents, run queries, generate podcasts — integrate it into CI/CD pipelines, automated research workflows, or custom UIs. NotebookLM has no API whatsoever.

Where NotebookLM Still Wins

1. Citations

NotebookLM highlights exact passages in the source document with click-to-navigate precision. Open Notebook gives source name + approximate location in text. If you're writing academic papers or legal briefs where "which paragraph did this come from" matters, NotebookLM's citation system is significantly better.

2. Zero Setup & Maintenance

You sign in and start. No Docker, no DNS, no TLS, no GPU sizing, no debugging Ollama OOM errors. For anyone who doesn't want to own infrastructure, NotebookLM wins instantly. Open Notebook requires comfort with the terminal.

3. Audio Overview Polish

NotebookLM's 2-speaker Audio Overviews have a natural conversational flow and "live host" feel that open-source TTS hasn't replicated yet. Open Notebook supports 1-4 speakers with editable prompts — more control, but the audio quality depends on your TTS provider and prompt tuning.
